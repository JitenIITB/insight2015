Academic Dishonesty

How honest were you with your academics?

23% of students were either dishonest throughout or when situation permitted!

In which of the following did you indulge in unfair means?

Believe it or not, but 92.8% of students resorted to unfair means at some point to solve assignments!
The figure falls to 42% for projects, presentations and quizzes and 33.8% for midsems and endsems.

What propelled you to use unfair means?

43.5% of students who were dishonest with academics, used unfair means because they lacked interest in their courses!

How ethically wrong do you think academic dishonesty is?

About 30% of students believe that although academic dishonesty is wrong, it is inescapable!

How would you rate the institute on the grounds of academic flexibility?

The mean rating of the academic flexibility in the institute stands only at a modest 7!
A rather meek rating for one of the top colleges in the country.

Had you been given an option to not attend class, how regularly would you have attended?

A staggering 37% of students said that they would attend classes rarely or just before exams, given the option to not attend classes!
Given the liberty, not ‘bunking’ would be an exception to the rule.

What was your primary reason for not wanting to attend classes?

More than 50% students did not want to attend classes either because classes were monotonous and professors were uninterested or because they didn’t learn anything!
A good 36.7% said they were too lazy to attend classes!

How much value addition do you think your stay at IIT-B provided you?

82.2% students felt that the learnt a lot from their stay at IIT-B and would carry these values ahead in life!
